Context

The current KNN implementation uses a frozen top-1 rule: the condition ranked first by the Bayesian layer can never be displaced by KNN neighbourhood evidence. This was a conservative safety choice to prevent noisy neighbours from overriding good Bayesian posteriors. However, the eval data shows this is leaving value on the table — particularly for kidney disease (0% ML top-1, Bayesian rescues to 46.5%) and prediabetes (18.6% final top-1 but 86.0% top-3).

Three strategies to A/B test: (1) Freeze top-1 always (current), (2) Unfreeze top-1 for low-confidence Bayesian posteriors (<0.60), (3) Unfreeze top-1 fully and rely on neighbourhood confidence alone.

Each question is assigned a value of information (VOI) score, and low-VOI questions are automatically flagged for human review every 30 days. Even without live retraining, maintaining an information gain leaderboard per question per condition — updated each eval run — creates this feedback loop manually.

EVAL-01
Eval Framework: Co-morbidity Cohort, Stratified Reporting & CI Integration
Eval Infra P2 Portfolio signal · catches regressions early Effort: Medium (6–8 hrs)
›

Context

The current eval framework is strong — 600-profile synthetic cohort, per-layer tracking, per-condition breakdowns — but has three gaps that limit its value as a portfolio artefact and regression safety net:

(1) No co-morbidity cohort: The 20 "edge case" co-morbid profiles in v3 are not systematically evaluated. Real users often have anemia + perimenopause, or prediabetes + inflammation, or thyroid + sleep disorder. The system's ability to handle co-morbid profiles — where two conditions should both appear in top-3 — is unmeasured.

(2) No stratified reporting: Top-1 accuracy is not broken out by sex, age group, BMI group, or quiz path (symptom-only vs. with labs). The gender bias in anemia and perimenopause was discovered manually, not automatically.

(3) No CI gate: Eval runs are manual. A regression (like the Bayesian sleep disorder −34pp) could be introduced undetected. A simple pytest or make eval check with hard thresholds per condition would catch this automatically.

Industry best practice

FDA AI/ML SaMD guidance and the NHS AI Lab evaluation framework both require subgroup performance reporting before clinical deployment. Even for a student project, stratified eval by sex and age is a significant portfolio differentiator — it demonstrates that you understand clinical ML fairness, not just aggregate accuracy. The co-morbidity eval is equally powerful: most real patients present with more than one relevant condition, so a system that only handles single-condition profiles is unrealistic.
