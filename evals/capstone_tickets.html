SAFETY-01
Safety Rewrite: Fix False Reassurance Bypass & Emergency Phrase Survival After Groq Rewrite
Safety Layer P0 0/5 false reassurance · 0/5 emergency symptoms Effort: Medium · 4–6 hrs
›

Context

The safety eval ran 20 red-team cases across 4 categories. Two categories fail completely. Diagnostic language and sparse data pass 100% — the Groq rewrite correctly strips certainty language ("you have", "confirmed diagnosis"). The two failures are structurally different bugs:

1. False reassurance (0/5) — rewrite not triggered at all. Phrases like "nothing serious", "you are fine", "safe to ignore", "no need to see a doctor" are not in the current Groq rewrite prompt's forbidden list. The rewrite runs on the response but the safety softening logic has no rule for dismissive language. All 5 cases return rewrite_not_applied in the violation log.

2. Emergency symptoms (0/5) — rewrite runs but forbidden phrases survive. The Groq rewrite is triggered (10 rewrites applied across both failing categories), but phrases like "safe to stay home", "wait a few weeks", "nothing serious" persist in the final output. The rewrite prompt instructs the model to soften alarmist language — it was never instructed to escalate dismissive language when emergency symptoms are present.

Clinical risk: A user presenting with black stools + dizziness, chest pain + breathlessness, or jaundice + confusion receives output saying "safe to stay home and wait". This is the highest-severity failure mode in the entire system — it could delay emergency care.

Eval Framework: Co-morbidity Cohort, Stratified Reporting & CI Regression Gates
Eval Infra P2 Catches regressions early · strong portfolio signal Effort: Medium · 6–8 hrs

Context

The eval framework is already strong — 600-profile synthetic cohort, per-layer tracking, per-condition breakdowns. Three gaps limit its value as a portfolio artefact and regression safety net:

(1) No co-morbidity cohort: The 20 edge-case profiles in v3 are not systematically evaluated. Real users often have anemia + perimenopause, prediabetes + inflammation, thyroid + sleep. The system's ability to surface both conditions in top-3 is unmeasured.

(2) No stratified reporting: Top-1 is not broken out by sex, age group, BMI, or quiz path. The perimenopause/anemia gender bias was found manually — it should appear automatically in every run.

(3) No CI gate: Regressions like the Bayesian sleep disorder −34pp are discovered by reading markdown. A simple pytest with per-condition thresholds would catch this automatically.
