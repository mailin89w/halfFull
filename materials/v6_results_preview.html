Areas worth checking

Iron deficiency Probable

Your 8-day heavy periods with clotting are the strongest clinical driver here — this is the most common cause of iron depletion in premenopausal women, and it was independently confirmed in your follow-up answers. Cold intolerance and cognitive fog are separate co-signals for low ferritin that your answers also confirmed.

Perimenopause Possible

At 38, the combination of cycle changes and night wakings raises perimenopause as a concurrent contributor. The evidence here doesn't confirm it — a hormone panel would — but it's distinct from the iron picture and worth ruling out with your GP or gynaecologist.

Next steps · talk to a doctor

Start with your GP for a full iron panel and hormone screen — both can be ordered in a single appointment. If ferritin is low, a gynaecologist is the next step to address the cause of blood loss, not just supplement it.
